Purab Pal

I am a cancer biologist with a research focus on breast and ovarian cancer, particularly on the role of lipid metabolism in endocrine therapy resistance. My work integrates molecular biology, cell biology, and biochemical approaches to understand how lipid-driven signaling and stress responses influence cancer progression and therapeutic vulnerability.

I am currently a postdoctoral research associate at the University of Illinois at Chicago, where I investigate lipid-mediated cellular mechanisms in estrogen receptor–positive breast cancer.

Beyond my academic research, I am also engaged in creative writing, music production, and science communication. This webpage serves as a record of my academic and para-academic work.

Web Resources

This section hosts a collection of web-based interactive resources developed to support routine laboratory work and to explore musical concepts through direct listening. The scientific tools focus on practical calculations and everyday workflows, whereas the musical tools are not intended as tutorials or evaluative software, but as experiential probes — inviting users to reflect on pitch, tuning, consonance, and related phenomena through listening.

Science

  • Primer designing
    Find candidate qPCR primers by gene name and organism, with basic quality checks.
  • Solution preparation
    Prepare molar, normal, or formal solutions from formula weight and target volume (including micro/nano/femto ranges).
  • qPCR data analysis
    Quickly visualize RT-qPCR data and compute fold change with basic default statistics for rapid inspection.

Music

  • Consonance Checker
    A web-based interactive tool for exploring the consonance or dissonance of two or more musical notes through direct auditory experience.
  • Blind Consonance Test
    Do you want to test if you can identify consonant musical intervals? Take this simple test.
  • Equal N Notes in an Octave
    Listen to how individual notes sound when you divide an octave into different parts.
  • Beating Explorer
    An interactive listening experiment designed to explore acoustic beating and perceptual consonance without relying on numbers or labels.

Popular-level Books

Gān-Bigyānér Kothā (“Of Music and Science”)
Anustup Prakashani, November 2025.

This book examines the deep and often overlooked relationship between music and science. Through essays that move from musical experience to technology and perception, the book explores how questions of “why” and “how” in music intersect with science.
